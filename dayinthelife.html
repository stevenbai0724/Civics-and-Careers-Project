A Day in the Life of a Software Developer

Most software developers work on weekdays, Monday to Friday, in a cozy, air conditioned and heated office.

Different companies start at different times but software developers usually get to work at 8-8:30 and start shortly after. Most of the work that software developers do involves coding.

For more information on what software developers do, go to the main page here

Developers usually have a meeting before working, debriefing the work that is to be done that day

Software developers enjoy a wide variety of activities at work. Other than just coding, they also attend meetings, write reports and help colleagues with computer related issues.

Stereotypically, software developers are antisocial and "nerdy" people but they are actually required to actively collaborate and discuss issues with co-workers and clients. Being a software developer can be a very interactive job!

Although some companies may have different rules and traditions, most software developers will take breaks after coding for a while to stretch and relieve stress, as well as to prevent any musculoskeletal issues related with posture.

Developers and many other coding-related workers take breaks every 2-3 hrs and do fun physical activities for a few mins such as playing ping pong, having a nerf war, etc.

Like many office jobs, software developers take a 30 min - 1 hr lunch break and continue working.

Developers end work at different times depending on the company, usually somewhere from 3:30 - 5:00.

Developers may have to do some coding outside of work but otherwise they live a very free lifestyle at home.
